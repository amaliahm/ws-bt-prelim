-MRI scans were originally 240×240×155 then transformed into 128×128×128 using random flipping and cropping, apply Z-score normalization
-Medical SAM-CLIP Grafting Network (MSCG): novel dl framework designed for fully automated brain tumor segmentation by combining the geometric precision of the (SAM) with the rich semantic context of CLIP
-CNNs and transformers which are commonly used have some limitation: 
CNNs have a limited receptive field
transformers capture global data but often lose local details resulting in blurred edges
foundation models like the original SAM lack medical domain knowledge and typically require manual prompts (like bounding boxes)
-the MSCG network utilizes a three-part architecture :
dual encoder: employs two parallel frozen encoders (SAM-Med3D and CT-CLIP) to process 4 MRI modalities (FLAIR, T1, T1ce,  T2)
SC-grafting module: grafts semantic information from the CLIP feature space into the SAM model at multiple layers (3, 6, 9, 12 transformer blocks)
fusion decoder: to generate the final output so it integrates semantic flow (upsampled encoder features) and detail flow (high-resolution edge data from raw images) then 3D mamba blocks which provide a global receptive field similar to Transformers but with much higher computational efficiency for 3D medical volumes.
-the model was trained and tested primarily on BraTS2023 (1251 samples) and validated on BraTS2018
