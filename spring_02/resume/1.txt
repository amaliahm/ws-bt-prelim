-in house dataset: 2100 MR scans belonging to 183 retrospective patients with high-grade gliomas who were scanned between 2004 and 2019 at the Lausanne University Hospital (average number of scans per subject 5, standard deviation 4.5), focused on the T2-weighted (T2w) scans. Scans that were too close to surgery (within 4 weeks) were excluded, extracted the radiology reports associated with each session, these were written (or dictated) in French during routine clinical practice by a junior radiologist after exploring all sequences of interest. Then, a senior radiologist reviewed each case amending the report when necessary. The extracted reports have varying length ranging from 121 to 751 words (average 325, standard deviation
84). The protocol of this study was approved by the regional ethics committee; written informed consent was waived. We release an anonymized version of our in-house dataset on Zenodo 
(DOI:10.5281/zenodo.7214605) under the permissive CC BY 4.0 license [56].
-split in house dataset to 2 sub dataset
-Human Annotated Dataset (HAD): generate 3 classes: 
Global conclusion( The overall takeaway of the report)
T1w conclusion (The evolution of the enhanced part of the tumor seen on T1-weighted sequences)
T2w conclusion (The evolution of the tumor specifically on T2-weighted sequences)
each class could be labeled by:
stable (the tumor did not change significantly with respect to the previous comparative exam)
progression (the tumor worsened with respect to the previous comparative exam)
response (the tumor responded positively to the treatment)
unknown (if the annotator was not able to assign any of the three classes above when reading the report)
-weakly annotated dataset (WAD): use NLP pipeline where they did preprocess (remove proper nouns, stopwords, punctuation), embedding (TF-IDF), classify (RandomForest) the radiology reports precisely into the classes (stable, progression, response)
-in house dataset:
initially 183 patients and 2100 scans
HAD 169 patients and 629 scans
WAD 170 patients and 1460 patients
-Image-Based Change Detection: parts of the tumor that either progress or respond to treatment (unstable) should appear as hyper-intense and if the tumor is stable across the two time-points so the difference map will likely be hypo-intense overall, it's generated by first applying N4 bias field correction with ANTs both tothe previous and to the current T2w volumes, then registered the previous scan to the current scan, again with ANTs, skull-stripped both volumes (previous warped and current) with HD-BET, applied z-score normalization on both volumes, computed the absolute voxel-wise difference of the normalized volumes.
-During training, they applied online data augmentations (flip) + Gaussian noise, zoom (0.7 to 1.3, 1 being the original volume size) and elastic deformation, each with probability of 20%.
-experiment two TL type to know which on eis the best (Baseline and TL) with VGG and SEResNeXt DL models
-Baseline experiment: conducted 5-fold cross-validation only on HAD, evaluation was performed on the test subjects of each cross-validation fold and then results were aggregated, the only two hyperparameters that were tuned for the Baseline experiment were learning rate and weight decay, former was chosen from {10-4, 10-5, 10-6}, latter was chosen from {0,0.01}., then run VGG-Baseline and SEResNeXt-Baseline experiments with all the six hyperparameter combinations.
-Transfer Learning (TL) experiment: performed 5-fold cross-validation on HAD and exploited the WAD difference maps. fine tuned 6 hyperparametes: learning rate, weight decay, transfer learning configuration, mixed training, feature extraction fraction of WAD where mixed training is boolean, fraction of WAD indicates which portion of WAD to use
